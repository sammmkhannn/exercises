Briards

by ivy Ducket

The briad, or berger de brie, is a large breed of dog traditionally used as a herder and gardian of sheep.

Breed History

The briard, which is believed to have originated in France, has been bred for centuries to herd and to protect sheep. The breed was used by the French Army as sentries, messengers and to search for wounded soldiers because of it's fine sense of hearing. Briards were used in the First World War almost to the poin of extinction. Currently the population of briards is slowly recovering. Charlemagne,Napoleon, Thomas Jefferson and Lafayette all owned briards.
